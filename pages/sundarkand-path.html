About This Path

Why Sundarkand is recited

Sundarkand, the fifth chapter of Tulsidas's Ramcharitmanas, follows Hanuman's journey to Lanka and is widely recited for courage, protection from obstacles, and resolution of prolonged difficulties.

The Sundarkand Path is performed with devotional focus and can be arranged for family groups or individual recitation, depending on the occasion and preference.

The session includes invocation, the full recitation with proper intonation, aarti, and closing prayers — performed either by the Pandit alone or with family members joining the chanting.

Inside the Ritual

What happens during Sundarkand Path, step by step

The structure we follow for this recitation.

  1. Sankalp & Hanuman Invocation

    The purpose of the path is stated, followed by an invocation to Hanuman with sindoor and diya lit before the idol or image.

  2. Opening Prayers

    Brief invocatory verses are recited to settle the space before the main text begins.

  3. Full Sundarkand Recitation

    The complete chapter is recited with proper intonation, covering Hanuman's journey to Lanka, his meeting with Sita, and the burning of Lanka.

  4. Group Chanting (Optional)

    Where family members wish to join, we pace the recitation so everyone can chant along.

  5. Aarti

    A closing aarti is performed, typically the Hanuman Chalisa or Aarti, with lamps lit before the image.

  6. Closing Blessings & Guidance

    The Pandit shares any specific guidance relevant to your situation before the session ends.
