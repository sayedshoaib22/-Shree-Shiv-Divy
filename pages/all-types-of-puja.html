Inside the Ritual

What generally happens during a puja, step by step

The exact sequence varies slightly by puja type — this is the common structure we follow.

  1. Sankalp (Intention Setting)

    The Pandit states your name, gotra, and the specific purpose of the puja, formally setting the ritual's intention.

  2. Ganesh & Kalash Invocation

    Lord Ganesh is invoked first to clear obstacles, followed by invocation into the kalash (sacred vessel) representing the presiding deity.

  3. Shodashopachara / Core Worship

    The sixteen (or a shortened set of) traditional offerings — water, flowers, incense, light, food — are made to the deity being worshipped.

  4. Mantra Recitation

    Relevant mantras or katha chapters are recited with correct pronunciation, at a pace you can follow along with if you wish.

  5. Aarti

    A closing aarti is performed with lit lamps, usually with the household joining in.

  6. Prasad & Visarjan

    Prasad is distributed to those present, and where relevant, guidance is given on visarjan (immersion) or safe-keeping of any idol or yantra used.

Getting Ready

What to keep ready before the Pandit arrives

A general checklist — we'll send a puja-specific version once your date is confirmed.

  • A clean, quiet space with enough room for the puja setup
  • A low wooden seat (chowki) or table for the deity/kalash
  • A working diya or lamp, and matches
  • Fresh flowers and, where possible, tulsi or bel leaves
  • A change of simple, clean clothing if you'll be seated through the ritual
  • Enough seating or floor space for all guests attending
