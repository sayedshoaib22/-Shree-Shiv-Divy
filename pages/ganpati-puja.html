About This Puja

Why Ganesh Puja is usually the first step

Lord Ganesh is invoked before most other rituals and important beginnings because he is regarded as the remover of obstacles. A Ganesh Puja is often performed before starting a business, moving into a new home, beginning construction, or launching any significant undertaking.

This centre performs Ganesh Puja with a traditional Vedic sequence, clear samagri guidance, and a calm, devotional style suited to home or temple settings.

The ceremony includes sankalp (intention setting), avahan (invocation), shodashopachara or a shorter abhishek sequence depending on your preference, aarti, and prasad distribution.

Inside the Ritual

What happens during your Ganesh Puja, step by step

The core sequence we follow — adjusted slightly for shorter or festival-day versions.

  1. Sankalp

    Your name, gotra, and the specific purpose of the puja — a new venture, festival, or home occasion — are formally stated.

  2. Avahan (Invocation)

    Lord Ganesh is invited into the idol or image with dedicated mantras, marking the beginning of the worship.

  3. Abhishek

    The murti is bathed with milk, honey, curd, and water in sequence, each offering carrying its own symbolic significance.

  4. Shodashopachara Worship

    Sixteen traditional offerings — from seating to sacred thread to food — are made, or a shortened version if you've chosen the quicker format.

  5. Aarti & Modak Offering

    A closing aarti is performed with lit lamps, and modak (Ganesh's favourite sweet) is offered before being shared as prasad.

  6. Visarjan Guidance

    If an idol was used for the occasion, we advise on the appropriate timing and method for visarjan.
