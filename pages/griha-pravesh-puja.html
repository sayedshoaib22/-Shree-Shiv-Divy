About This Puja

Why the timing matters as much as the ritual

Griha Pravesh marks the first formal entry into a new or renovated home, and traditional practice places real weight on choosing the correct muhurat — the date and time that supports a smooth, settled beginning for the household.

We review the home or property context where relevant, recommend a suitable muhurat, and coordinate the puja setup with attention to Vastu and practical convenience.

The ceremony typically includes Vastu Puja, Ganesh and Navagraha invocation, a symbolic boiling-over of milk at the threshold, and a Satyanarayan Katha where the household wishes to combine both rituals in one visit.

Inside the Ritual

What happens during Griha Pravesh Puja, step by step

The structure we follow for a new home entry.

  1. Vastu Puja

    The property itself is honoured first, acknowledging the Vastu Purush associated with the land and structure.

  2. Ganesh & Navagraha Invocation

    Ganesh is invoked for an obstacle-free beginning, followed by a Navagraha invocation to settle planetary influence over the home.

  3. Kalash Sthapana

    A sacred kalash is placed at the entrance or puja area, representing auspicious energy entering the home.

  4. Threshold Rituals

    Milk is symbolically boiled over on the stove for the first time, and the entrance is marked with mango leaves and rangoli as the family crosses in.

  5. Griha Pravesh Homam

    A small havan is performed within the home to formally consecrate the space.

  6. Satyanarayan Katha (If Combined)

    Where booked together, the full Katha recitation follows, ending in shared aarti and prasad for all guests.
