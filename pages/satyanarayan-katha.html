About This Katha

Why families choose Satyanarayan Katha

Satyanarayan Katha is one of the most commonly performed household pujas, often held to mark a birthday, anniversary, house-warming, recovery from illness, business success, or simply as a periodic expression of gratitude.

The Katha is conducted in a devotional, structured manner with clear chanting, aarti, and flexible arrangements for family groups or smaller gatherings.

The ceremony includes the panchamrit abhishek, the five-chapter Katha recitation, aarti, and distribution of the signature prasad (sheera/halwa) to all those present.

Inside the Ritual

What happens during Satyanarayan Katha, step by step

The traditional structure we follow for this recitation.

  1. Sankalp & Ganesh Invocation

    The occasion and your intention are stated, followed by a brief invocation to Lord Ganesh before the main Katha begins.

  2. Panchamrit Abhishek

    Lord Satyanarayan (a form of Vishnu) is bathed with the five sacred substances — milk, curd, honey, ghee, and sugar.

  3. Five-Chapter Katha Recitation

    The complete story is recited across its five chapters, each carrying a lesson about faith, gratitude, and consequence — narrated so guests can follow along.

  4. Aarti

    A closing aarti is performed with lit lamps, usually with all guests joining in the singing.

  5. Prasad (Sheera) Distribution

    The signature sheera/halwa prasad, prepared with banana and semolina, is distributed to everyone present as the final step.

  6. Closing Blessings

    The Pandit shares any specific guidance relevant to your occasion before concluding the session.

Getting Ready

What to keep ready before Satyanarayan Katha

A general checklist to help the session run smoothly.

  • A clean puja area with space for the kalash and offerings
  • Banana leaves or a small banana plant stem, if available
  • Ingredients for sheera/halwa prasad (semolina, ghee, sugar, banana)
  • Seating arranged for all expected guests
  • A working diya, incense, and matches
  • A change of simple clothing for those closely involved in the ritual
