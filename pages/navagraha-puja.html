Inside the Ritual

What happens during Navagraha Puja, step by step

The structure we follow once the puja has been confirmed as relevant to your chart.

  1. Sankalp

    Your specific chart concern — the graha or dosha being addressed — is formally stated as the puja's intention.

  2. Ganesh Invocation

    As with most pujas, Lord Ganesh is invoked first to support an obstacle-free ritual.

  3. Navagraha Mandala/Yantra Setup

    For the full version, the nine planetary positions are set up with their associated grains, colours, and directions.

  4. Individual Graha Worship

    Each relevant planet is worshipped in sequence with its own mantra, offering, and colour-specific item, or just the afflicted graha(s) for a focused session.

  5. Havan (Where Applicable)

    For stronger doshas, a small havan (fire ritual) may be included — this is confirmed with you in advance as it changes setup requirements.

  6. Aarti & Closing Guidance

    A closing aarti is performed, followed by the Pandit's guidance on any daan (charity) or additional practice to support the remedy.

Getting Ready

What to keep ready before Navagraha Puja

A general checklist — full requirements depend on the scope confirmed after your chart review.

  • Your accurate date, time, and place of birth for the chart review
  • A clean, spacious area if a full mandala setup is planned
  • Nine types of grains (til, rice, wheat, moong, chana, etc.) if arranging samagri yourself
  • A working diya, incense, and matches
  • Comfortable seating for the duration of the puja, which can run longer than other rituals
